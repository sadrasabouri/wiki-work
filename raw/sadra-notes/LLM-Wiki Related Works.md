The idea for creating a second brain is a long history concept were people want to have a digital memory of what they capture which resonate with them, organize their thoughts once in a while , distil information from it, and express ideas as needed (CODE method: [[Building a Second Brain The Definitive Introductory Guide]]). Recently creating a wiki out of a raw data information using LLM agents like claude is used by [[Post by @karpathy on X]]. He shared a prompt which people can use to convert any fleeting notes of them to extract entities out. Entities then connected to each other by their relations and form a wiki view over the information.
Since then people applied the idea into different domain such as finance ([[Claude + Obsidian have to be illegal]]), manager summaries ([[garrytangbrain Garry's Opinionated OpenClawHermes Agent Brain]]), tracking personal vibe sessions ([[cianmarboLore Lore is a living knowledge base powered by Claude Code sessions.]]).
One important distinction that such a system has is its ability to retrieve required information for grounded tasks for agents in a much efficient way than RAG systems ([[cagataysengorllm-wiki-studio A productized RAG app that turns documents and code files into a searchable wiki and AI Q&A workspace.]]) and showing that using such an structure could lead into token usage up to 99% ([[radimsemremindb An agentic memory database that cuts session tokens by 75–99%. One portable SQLite file — your agent's memory, anywhere.]]).

# Related Work
## For Software Developers
The cold-start problem for software developers when they need to feed in context on every interaction with coding agent e.g,. on who are they what are the projects they're working on, how different projects relates to each other, and design decisions made that later on forget. Additionally noting down a new technic or, new library they found that they may forget about when they actually need it, or a new college they want to follow their work. These are pain-points could be resolved if there was a consistent structural personal memory that they could point the agent to as a starter. Some examples of such projects using [[Post by @karpathy on X]]'s idea on software developers are:

## For Researchers
Researchers are full of ideas. There are so many "What if we had ..." thoughts that may take minutes of her day pumping up dopamine of the interesting aspects of the idea. These insight moments can happen everywhere, when reading a research paper, when attending conferences, when a college uses a specific framing for a subject that inspires other over a coffee break. After facing [[Post by @karpathy on X]]'s workflow these people asked "What if we had a second brain that could save all these random thoughts collected through years and show me the interesting connection helping me to understand what common concepts worth exploring? What is the missing research question answering which result into a bigger picture of my research thesis? How I can get more insights?" and developed several projects to achieve these goals:

## Critics
+ Since conversion from raw source to wiki format is lossy this is not the ultimate solution. ([[Anatoly Krasnovsky's comment on llm-wiki limitations]])
+ There's a need for wiki-based systems to be benchmarked against RAG ([[Anatoly Krasnovsky's comment on llm-wiki limitations]])
+ In scale when graph gets bigger the tasks became untrivial and maintaining the graph would be a big issue ([[Anatoly Krasnovsky's comment on llm-wiki limitations]])
