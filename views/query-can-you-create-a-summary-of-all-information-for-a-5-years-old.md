# Everything in This Wiki — for a 5-Year-Old

## The Big Idea

Imagine you and your friends are building a really big Lego castle together. Every day you add new pieces, but sometimes you forget what you built last week, or what your friend built in a different room. **This project is like a magic helper that remembers everything about the castle, so nobody gets confused.**

The magic helper is called a **Knowledge Base** (or KB). It reads all your notes and meeting recordings and turns them into a tidy notebook that everyone can look at.

---

## The Two Things Being Built

### 1. The Smart Notebook ([[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]])
[[sadra-sabouri|Sadra]], [[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]], and [[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]] are building a notebook that:
- Reads all your old notes and meeting recordings automatically
- Writes nice summaries so you don't have to read everything again
- Can show the same information in lots of different ways — like how a story can be told as a book, a movie, or a puppet show. These different ways of showing things are called **[[views|Views]]**.

The coolest part: the notebook already *contains* all the notes about how the notebook was built. It's like a storybook that tells the story of how it was written! ([[self-referential-kb|Self-Referential KB]])

### 2. The Spreadsheet Helper ([[spreadsheet-verification|Spreadsheet Verification]])
When a computer helper (an AI) fills in a big table of numbers, how do you know it didn't make a mistake? [[sadra-sabouri|Sadra]], [[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]], [[sujay-maladi|Sujay]], and [[athena-saghi|Athena]] built a tool that lets you:
- **Stop** the computer in the middle of its work
- **See** a picture of all the steps it took (like a family tree of calculations — [[dag-of-computation|DAG of Computation]])
- **Steer** it if it made a wrong turn ([[steerability|Steerability]])

They call this **"stop, see, steer"** — or [[agent-lens|Agent Lens]].

## The Magic Word: Views

The most important idea in the whole project is **[[views|Views]]**. A view is just a way of asking: *"Show me this information, but only the part I care about, in the shape I need."*

- A teacher might want a **summary**.
- A boss might want a **presentation**.
- A new friend might want a **simple explanation** (like this one!).
- A researcher might want a **list of open questions**.

The same notes can make all of these. That's a view. [[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]] noticed that views are the big idea that connects *both* projects — the notebook AND the spreadsheet helper.

## A Few Surprising Things

- **AI makes you faster but less creative** ([[productivity-creativity-tradeoff|Productivity-Creativity Tradeoff]]): [[athena-saghi|Athena]] found that when people use AI to help them, they finish faster — but they have fewer fun "I just thought of something amazing!" moments.
- **Private notes can be shared carefully** ([[semi-private-mashing|Semi-Private Mashing]]): If you and a friend have a private chat, you can share *just the useful parts* with the whole group, without sharing the private stuff.
- **The notebook knows when it's wrong** ([[purge-operation|Purge Operation]]): When new information shows that something old was incorrect, the notebook can forget the old thing and remember the new thing instead.
- **The ultimate dream** ([[digital-twins|Digital Twins]]): One day the notebook might know a person so well — their ideas, how they think, even their feelings — that it's almost like talking to that person directly.
