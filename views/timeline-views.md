### Key Inflection Points

- **2026-03-16** — The naming moment: Sumit intervened on terminology mid-meeting and reframed step-by-step reveal from a UI feature into an instance of a broader abstraction — "views." This single move elevated the concept from a project-specific affordance to the unifying idea across two distinct research projects. Agent Lens, steerability, and the DAG view all clicked into place around this reframe.
- **2026-05-01** — Taxonomy arrives: the first meeting where concrete view types (design doc, contribution review, topic summary) were enumerated, and where hierarchical composition (views that contain sub-views) was articulated. Views moved from a named abstraction to a designable system.
- **2026-05-02** — Conceptual boundary drawn: separating views (declarative) from workflows (constructive) gave views a precise definition rather than an expansive one, and grounded it in a database analogy (saved query over mutable data). The purge and provenance concepts clarified what views operate over — a temporally coherent, human-verified corpus.

---

Views began as an unnamed observation: step-by-step reveal and granular feedback were useful affordances for spreadsheet verification. Sumit's intervention on March 16 named the abstraction and immediately linked it across two projects — a rare moment where one word restructured a research agenda. Between March and May, views accumulated specificity: a three-part structure (prompt, workflow, data selection), a growing taxonomy of types, and a formal distinction from workflows. By May 2 the concept was stable enough to be self-referential — the wiki's own slash commands are views, and the presentation about the research is itself a view applied to the KB. Views now stand as the central organizing concept of the Wiki KB Project, the lens through which both spreadsheet verification and knowledge management are unified.
