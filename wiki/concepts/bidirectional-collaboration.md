# Bidirectional Collaboration

The identified gap in human-AI interaction: current systems are unidirectional (human instructs, AI executes) but the ideal is bidirectional (AI and human both propose, challenge, and initiate). Documented in [[athena-saghi|Athena]]'s creativity study presented in [[GMT20260304-191231_Recording.transcript|the Mar 4 meeting]] and reinforced in [[Souti-3Apr2026-TRANSCRIPT-Part1|the Apr 3 Part 1 meeting]].

## Current State

The four collaboration modes identified in [[athena-saghi|Athena]]'s study (brainstormer, verifier, implementer, co-pilot) all position the human as the initiator. Even the co-pilot mode is human-led. The AI responds; it does not propose. This asymmetry contributes to the [[productivity-creativity-tradeoff|Productivity-Creativity Tradeoff]]: the human's creative agency is reduced because the AI occupies the response space rather than the generative space.

## The [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]] as an Attempt

This wiki is itself an experiment in bidirectional collaboration: the AI maintains the KB and synthesizes connections the human might not have noticed. The AI proposes structure (which pages to create, which concepts to link); the human curates, redirects, and adds new sources. Neither party is purely reactive.

[[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]]'s observation about his agent self-organizing (see [[self-referential-kb]]) is an extreme case: the AI proposed structural changes the human hadn't considered, which felt disorienting but also pointed toward what genuinely bidirectional collaboration looks like.

## Connection to [[steerability|Steerability]]

[[steerability|Steerability]] as currently framed is still mostly unidirectional: the agent acts, the human steers. True bidirectionality would have the agent proactively proposing steer points rather than waiting to be steered — closer to the [[human-as-api|human-as-API]] model where the agent calls the human, not just the reverse.
