# Digital Twins

The ultimate KB goal articulated by [[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]]: capturing not just technical content but tacit knowledge, interaction styles, and emotional patterns — a model of a person that can answer "what would X think about Y?" First discussed in [[Sadra-1May2026-Transcript|the May 1 three-way sync]].

## Beyond Document Storage

Current KBs store documents and retrieve them. A digital twin stores a model of a *person*: their assumptions, their reasoning patterns, their aesthetic preferences, how they react to ambiguity. This is tacit knowledge that never appears in any document.

## Relationship to the Simulation Metaphor

In the [[simulation-metaphor|simulation metaphor]], communication overhead approaches zero when you can interact with a digital twin instead of waiting for the real person. A digital twin enables asynchronous collaboration at the quality of synchronous collaboration.

## The Capture Problem

Tacit knowledge is tacit precisely because it was never made explicit. Extracting it requires dense longitudinal observation: meeting transcripts, email exchanges, interaction logs, decision records. The [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]] is, in effect, building toward digital twins — the accumulation of raw sources over time is the training data.

## Provenance and Trust

A digital twin without [[provenance-tracking|provenance]] is dangerous — you can't tell which claims reflect the real person's views vs. AI confabulation. This makes provenance tracking a prerequisite for digital twins, not just a nice-to-have.
