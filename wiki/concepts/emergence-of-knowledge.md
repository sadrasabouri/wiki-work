# Emergence of Knowledge

A class of network-analytic methods, introduced by [[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]] in [[T15-2026-05-06|the May 6 sync]], for modeling when groups are *close to discovering new ideas* — not just summarizing what was discussed, but predicting what is about to emerge across team boundaries. Souti referenced ICLR papers (one published last year) that formalize:

- **Divergence and convergence metrics** across networks of teams or concepts
- **Link fragility** — how easily a connection between two nodes (teams, concepts) breaks
- **Emergence prediction** — when two networks are close to converging on a new idea that has not yet been discussed

Sumit's reaction was unusually strong: *"This is the most interesting thing I have learned in a long time."* He asked Souti to send the papers and added emergence as an explicit creative-view target for cross-team transcripts.

## Why It Matters for the Wiki Project

Today's wiki summarizes what *was* discussed. Emergence-of-knowledge inverts the question: across a corpus of meetings, which sub-networks are close to producing something novel that hasn't yet been said? This is a **predictive** view, not a descriptive one — and it requires the cross-team data scope that [[wiki-kb-project|the wiki project]] aspires to ([[semi-private-mashing|semi-private mashing]]).

It also generalizes Sumit's earlier formulation of [[views|views]] as creative outputs over a corpus: a view can ask not only "what is here?" but "what is *almost* here?"

## Connections to Other Concepts

- **[[steerability]]** — knowing where ideas are about to emerge gives the user a steer point at the *organization* scale, not just the workflow scale.
- **[[contradiction-mining]]** *(not yet a page)* — Sumit's interest in surfacing contradictions and unspoken questions across transcripts is the descriptive cousin of emergence prediction; both ask the corpus what humans missed.
- **[[gulf-of-envisioning]]** — emergence-prediction is itself an example of a view people don't know to ask for. It belongs in the gallery of creative views ([[skills]]) so users discover it.

## Open Questions

- The ICLR papers Souti referenced have not yet been added to `raw-sources/` — they are a TODO from the meeting.

- The "right question to ask" — Sumit linked this to Elon Musk's framing "the answer is forty-two, but what is the question?" — is itself a steerability problem: AI can compute many emergence metrics, but a domain-aware human must select which ones matter.
