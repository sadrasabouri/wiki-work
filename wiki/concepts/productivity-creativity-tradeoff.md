# Productivity-Creativity Tradeoff

LLMs make users more productive — more correct, more complete work — but reduce the time spent on idea generation and the number of "aha moments." Documented by [[athena-saghi|Athena Saghi]] in her creativity study, presented in [[GMT20260304-191231_Recording.transcript|the Mar 4 meeting]].

## The Finding

Users with LLM assistance completed tasks faster and with higher quality outputs. But they generated fewer novel ideas and reported fewer moments of spontaneous insight. The AI fills the incubation space that would otherwise generate unexpected connections.

## Wallace Creativity Model

The study used the Wallace model of creativity: preparation → incubation → illumination → verification. LLMs help most with preparation and verification (structured, evaluable) but compress or bypass incubation (unstructured, generative). Illumination — the "aha moment" — depends on incubation time.

## Four Collaboration Modes

1. **Brainstormer** — AI generates ideas, human selects
2. **Verifier** — AI checks human work
3. **Implementer** — AI executes human-specified plan
4. **Co-pilot** — bidirectional real-time collaboration

Current tools support 1–3 well. Mode 4 (co-pilot) requires [[bidirectional-collaboration|bidirectional collaboration]] and is largely unsolved. See also [[Souti-3Apr2026-TRANSCRIPT-Part1|the Apr 3 Part 1 meeting]] where Souti frames this as "AI supporting human creativity vs. AI closing the creativity gap."

## Connection to the Wiki Project

The [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]] faces this tradeoff directly: if the AI maintains and synthesizes the KB, does the researcher lose the creative insight that comes from manually connecting ideas? [[views|Views]] are one mitigation — the human defines what they want to see, preserving the creative framing work.
