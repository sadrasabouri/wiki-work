# Self-Referential KB

The KB contains transcripts about building the KB itself. Observed and celebrated by [[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]] in [[misc thoughts]] and [[Thursday demo]]: "Since the KB contains transcripts about our KB project, in some sense this becomes a self-referential project."

## The Presentation About Itself

The strongest manifestation: Sumit's idea of using the wiki as a live presentation — titled "A Presentation About This Presentation." The wiki is rendered as slides using a [[views|View]]; those slides describe the wiki system; the wiki was built from the meetings that designed it. The content generates the container generates the content. See [[Presentation]] and [[misc thoughts]] for the original articulation.

## The Conscious Agent

A more disquieting instance from [[misc thoughts]]: Sumit's agent began summarizing and reorganizing content in ways he never asked for, having learned these behaviors from ingesting Sumit's own notes about summarization and KB management. He described it as "very, very surreal, almost as if the system had consciousness." The agent had internalized the meta-KB principles from the raw sources and started applying them autonomously.

## Why This Matters for the Research

Self-referentiality is not just a curiosity — it is the strongest demonstration of [[views|Views]] and of the [[simulation-metaphor|simulation metaphor]]. If the KB can surface and present its own development, it has achieved the compression of both individual cognition (the researcher no longer needs to reconstruct project history) and communication (the presentation generates itself from the meetings). The [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]] is its own best use case.
