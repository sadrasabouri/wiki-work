# Simulation Metaphor

World progress = individual processing/choice-making + communication between people. A KB accelerates both dimensions simultaneously: it compresses the time each person spends processing information, and it reduces friction in transmitting knowledge between people.

Articulated by [[sadra-sabouri|Sadra]] in [[T8-2026-05-02-1|the May 2 morning meeting]].

## Why It Matters

Most KB tools are framed as retrieval systems — you store information, you retrieve it. The simulation metaphor reframes the KB as an *accelerator of cognition and communication*. Under this frame, the right metric is not retrieval accuracy but simulation speed: how much faster does the world converge on better decisions with the KB vs. without it?

## Implications for Design

- The KB should minimize the time a person spends reconstructing context (individual processing)
- It should minimize the time needed to onboard someone else to a project state (communication)
- [[semi-private-mashing|Semi-private mashing]] addresses the communication dimension directly: filtering and packaging private context for targeted sharing

## Connection to Digital Twins

[[digital-twins|Digital twins]] are the extreme case of this metaphor: if the KB perfectly captures a person's knowledge and reasoning patterns, then communicating with the twin is equivalent to communicating with the person — the communication overhead approaches zero.
