# Stories

A **story** is an atomic personal anecdote — a discrete life event preserved in detail and tagged for later reuse. Stories are the highest-priority content type in [[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]]'s personal wiki ([[T15-2026-05-06|May 6 sync]]), more important than concepts, projects, or people pages.

## Why Stories Belong in a Wiki

Stories are reusable across radically different contexts. Sumit's example: a kindergarten drop-off where he watched his son hop away from the car with his backpack and started crying in the Tesla — which then complained "pay attention to the road." He later used the same anecdote in a scientific talk on AI edge cases (the system can't distinguish closed eyes from teary eyes). **Same atomic story, two contexts.** Without a personal wiki to surface it, the story would not have come to mind when needed.

Sumit's claim: he has "close to 50 to 100 stories" scattered across his computer that he wants to recover before they're lost. The stakes are high enough that he called it "probably one last time in my life" — preserving them is non-negotiable.

## What Stories Are Not

- **Not meeting summaries.** Meeting transcripts are raw sources; the wiki distills them into concepts and decisions. A story is a single life event, not a summary of an interaction.
- **Not abstractions.** "Edge cases in AI systems are hard" is a concept; the kindergarten drop-off is a story that *illustrates* the concept. The wiki holds both, separately.
- **Not pre-tagged for use.** Tagging a story by which talks it could fit *constrains* its reusability. The retrieval system should surface stories by current need, not by pre-declared application.

## Place in the Personal Wiki

In Sumit's articulation in [[T15-2026-05-06|May 6]], the personal-wiki content priority is:

1. **Stories** — atomic anecdotes
2. **Analogies** — Sumit's own analogies (he wants to attach to *his* analogies, not others')
3. **Interesting visualizations and slide titles** — six-word slide-title stories, picture ideas, formatting moves
4. **Concepts and people** — only after the above

This inverts the default org-wiki priority (concepts first). The personal wiki is closer to a *creative writer's* notebook than to an encyclopedia.

## Stories vs. the Org-Wiki Layer

Stories live primarily in the **personal** layer of the [[local-first-kb|local-first KB]] model. Most stories should not promote to the org wiki — they're idiosyncratic by design. But certain stories (e.g., a project anecdote that everyone in the team witnessed) might cross via the [[semi-private-mashing|semi-private mashing]] mechanism.

## Open Question

Stories are stronger when surfaced *by need* — when preparing a talk on edge cases, the kindergarten story should rise to the top. Today's wiki retrieval is keyword/concept based; story retrieval may need a separate index keyed on emotional valence, narrative shape, or implicit themes. Souti raised the analogous emergence-of-knowledge question for cross-team networks ([[emergence-of-knowledge]]); a story-retrieval skill is the personal-wiki counterpart.
