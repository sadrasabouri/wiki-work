# Mar 4, 2026 — Athena's Creativity Study Presentation

[[athena-saghi|Athena Saghi]] presented findings from her study on LLMs and creativity. Raw transcript: [[T2-2026-03-04-1912]].

**Attendees:** [[sadra-sabouri]], [[souti-chattopadhyay]], [[athena-saghi]] and others

## Key Finding

[[productivity-creativity-tradeoff|Productivity-Creativity Tradeoff]]: LLM assistance produced more correct, more complete work but reduced "aha moments" and time spent on idea generation. The gain in productivity comes at a cost to creative insight.

## Theoretical Frameworks Used

- **Wallace creativity model**: preparation → incubation → illumination → verification. LLMs compress or bypass the *incubation* phase — the unstructured generative time that produces unexpected connections.
- **Torrance creativity tests**: divergent thinking (generating many ideas) followed by convergent thinking (selecting/refining).

## Four Collaboration Modes

Brainstormer, verifier, implementer, co-pilot. Current AI systems handle the first three. Mode 4 (co-pilot / bidirectional) is the open problem — see [[bidirectional-collaboration]].

## What This Changed

Framed the creativity problem as distinct from the productivity problem — directly informing [[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]]'s framing in [[2026-04-03|the Apr 3 meetings]] of "two AI roles: supporting human creativity vs. closing the creativity gap."
