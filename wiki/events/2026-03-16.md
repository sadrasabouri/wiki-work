# Mar 16, 2026 — Steerability Coined; Views Unified

The most conceptually dense meeting across both projects. Raw transcript: [[GMT20260316-200648_Recording.transcript]].

**Attendees:** [[sadra-sabouri]], [[sumit-gulwani]], [[souti-chattopadhyay]]

## What Happened

[[sadra-sabouri|Sadra]] presented formative study results (8 participatory design sessions on step-by-step reveal and "granular feedback on edit").

[[sumit-gulwani|Sumit]] intervened on the terminology: renamed "granular feedback" to [[steerability|steerability]] — "the user is not just giving feedback... they want to steer the output in the direction they would like."

Sumit then reframed the entire project: "the big, big idea is not a step-by-step reveal — it is providing different kinds of [[views|views]] into the output of the agent." He explicitly named the [[dag-of-computation|DAG view]] alongside step-by-step reveal as a second key view.

The explicit connection: Sumit identified [[views|Views]] as the abstraction that unifies [[agent-traceability-steerability|Spreadsheet Verification]] and the [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]].

## Concepts Introduced or Named

- [[steerability]] (replaces "granular feedback")
- [[agent-lens|Agent Lens]] ("stop, see, steer" framework name)
- [[dag-of-computation|DAG of computation]] (as a key view)
- [[gulf-of-envisioning|Gulf of Envisioning]] (preloaded Q&A as mitigation)
- [[views]] as unifying concept across both projects

## Key Observation from Souti

[[souti-chattopadhyay|Souti]] noted that the agent "takes over control" — an early articulation from the user's side of the problem that [[steerability]] was designed to solve.
