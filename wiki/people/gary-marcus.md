# Gary Marcus

NYU professor and prominent AI critic. Published a widely-circulated LinkedIn post on 2026-05-02 arguing that Generative AI has been a net negative for society — cited in [[Presentation]] by Sumit Gulwani as external framing for the [[wiki-kb-project|Wiki KB Project]].

## Core Claim

Outside of **coding** and a handful of other domains (e.g. **brainstorming**), GenAI has been net negative. The harms Marcus lists: undermining education, enabling mass surveillance, amplifying disinformation and deepfakes, facilitating phishing and cybercrime, introducing bias in employment, widening economic disparity, and imposing environmental costs through data centers. The structural problem: gains privatized, downsides socialized, with "almost zero responsibility" taken by those who profit.

Root causes: technical (LLMs aren't reliable) and political/economic (absence of responsible regulation). Not fully anti-AI — Marcus says some future form of AI might be great, but the current generation has "hurt more than it has helped."

Source: [[AI Backlash Grows Amidst Negative Impact on Society  Gary Marcus posted on the topic]]

## Relevance to This KB

Sumit forwarded this post in [[Presentation]] with the observation: "Our wiki kinda allows us to 'brainstorming'." The move is strategic: Marcus's critique defines a narrow legitimacy band for GenAI (coding + brainstorming), and the wiki project operates squarely within that band. A harsh skeptic's critique becomes positioning support — the wiki doesn't need to rebut Marcus; it fits inside what he already grants.

This is distinct from the [[productivity-creativity-tradeoff|productivity-creativity tradeoff]] concern (which is about creativity suppression within GenAI's productive domains). Marcus's critique is about whether GenAI domains should exist at all; his brainstorming carve-out is the relevant claim here.
