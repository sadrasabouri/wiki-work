# Presentation: Grounded Examples — Views, Org Brain, Semi-Private Mashup

## Views, Not Documents

1. **Teams meeting auto-summary as a proto-view — and what comes next** — Teams already auto-generates a post-meeting summary: one fixed template, one fixed algorithm, no user control. This work extends this to user-definable templates and per-topic queries composable across meetings. Concretely: during the MS Teams testing, Sadra ran "give me contradictions between two people across all meetings" — a query no fixed template can answer. The Teams summary shows the market exists and the pattern is understood; the KB shows where it goes. *Sumit Gulwani — [[Transcripts]]*

2. **Pista project: same computation, three different views** — In the spreadsheet agent tool, a single agent computation is rendered as a step-by-step reveal for debugging, a contribution summary for review, and an overview for inspection. Sumit identified this as the moment views unified both projects: "the big, big idea is not a step-by-step reveal — it is providing different kinds of views into the output of the agent." *Sumit Gulwani — [[Auditing and Controlling AI Agent Actions in Spreadsheets]]*

3. **Sumit's "connect" (performance review) as a view** — Sumit spent 3 days trying to auto-generate his annual performance review. He supplied all direct reports' connects, 1:1 transcripts, email Q&A threads, past examples, blog posts, paper lists, and hand-written guidance — and still got slop. Root cause: no skill told the agent *how* to prioritize and synthesize. The fix: author the connect as a view where each section (story, definitions, examples) is a prompt guided by a skill. What makes this example interesting for an external audience: it is non-IP information that gives a concrete picture of how a senior practitioner's workflow breaks without the system. *Sumit Gulwani — [[Sadra-5May2026-TRANSCRIPT2]]*

4. **This presentation, generated from the wiki** — Sadra ran `/presentation` over the KB and showed Sumit the result live. The slide outline cited the simulation metaphor, semi-private mashing, and contribution-map view — all pulled from meetings, not written from scratch. Sumit's diagnosis: the KB's knowledge layer gave the agent structured material to reason over, not raw text to rediscover from zero. The self-referential case: a presentation about the wiki, generated by the wiki. *Sadra Sabouri + Sumit Gulwani — [[Sadra-5May2026-TRANSCRIPT2]]*

## The Organizational Brain

1. **"Which AI trend was discussed most in Microsoft today?"** — Sumit framed this as the canonical CEO-level question. No individual can answer it. No search tool returns it. An org-brain grounded in that day's transcripts across all of Microsoft can. The permission scope required is exactly what Satya already has — "Satya, imagine your power when the Microsoft brain comes up." *Sumit Gulwani — [[Sadra-5May2026-TRANSCRIPT2]]*

2. **Connecting two people working on the same hidden problem** — "Identify two people in different organizations working on the same hidden problem and put them in touch." No org chart, no directory, no search returns this — it requires understanding the *content* of what people are working on across organizational boundaries. *Sumit Gulwani — [[Sadra-5May2026-TRANSCRIPT2]]*

3. **Preventing rework across teams** — "Tons of rework is happening that wouldn't be there if there was a common source of updating knowledge where individuals can contribute using their semi-private information." When private insights stay locked in personal KBs, teams independently solve the same problems. The org brain surfaces solved problems before a second team begins solving them — but only if individuals can contribute selectively from their private context. Work IQ indexes the same M365 data and cannot do this. *Sadra Sabouri — [[Microsoft Tool For Idea Automation]]*

## Semi-Private Mashup

1. **Agent pulls three sentences of unpublished work** — Sumit's agent, given access to a draft he was helping a colleague with, pulled three sentences of her unpublished work into his own output. He caught it manually and asked her to remove it. This is the failure mode: raw information used directly, with no wiki filter in the path. The KB must make this impossible by default — filtering before content enters the shared layer, not after. *Sumit Gulwani — [[Sadra-5May2026-TRANSCRIPT2]]*

2. **Souti's 1:1 concern: the design constraint that shaped the system** — In the May 1 three-way sync, Souti raised the concern explicitly: 1:1 transcripts contain sensitive content that should never appear in a shared KB. This forced the team to design semi-private mashing as a first-class operation rather than a workaround. Privacy shapes the architecture, not the other way around. *Souti Chattopadhyay — [[Sadra-1May2026-Transcript]]*

3. **Grounded theory as the filter: extracting themes, not transcripts** — A concrete example of how semi-private mashing works when the skill is specified: Sadra applies grounded theory analysis (inductive coding → clustering → naming) as a skill to a private 1:1 transcript with Sumit. The skill extracts concept clusters — "views", "skills", "privacy concerns" — without exposing the raw conversation. Only the thematic map enters the shared KB; the transcript stays local. The filter is not a redaction rule; it is a methodological transformation. The output is original synthesis, not a sanitized excerpt. *Sadra Sabouri — [[skills]]*
